E-sports

E-sports, short for electronic sports, are competitive video gaming events where
players or teams compete against each other in various video games.It has rapidly
grown into a global phenomenon, attracting millions of players and viewers worldwide.

Evolution and Growth

E-sports have evolved from small local competitions to large-scale international events
held in arenas and stadiums. The growth of live streaming platforms and social media
has further fueled the popularity of E-sports, making it accessible to a global audience.

Professional E-sports Scene

Professional E-sports organizations, teams, and players compete at the highest level, with tournaments offering substantial prize pools and lucrative sponsorships. The dedication and skill of professional players have elevated E-sports to a legitimate form of competitive entertainment. Major events like The International for Dota 2 and the League of Legends World Championship attract millions of viewers, both online and in packed arenas. E-sports athletes often undergo rigorous training regimes, similar to traditional sports, to maintain peak performance and stay competitive.

Popular E-sports Games

Battlegrounds Mobile India (BGMI)

Battlegrounds Mobile India, also known as BGMI, is a popular battle royale game developed by Krafton. It is the Indian version of PUBG Mobile, offering similar gameplay with exclusive events and content tailored for Indian players.

League of Legends

League of Legends, commonly known as LoL, is a highly popular MOBA game developed by Riot Games. In LoL, players control characters called "champions" with unique abilities and compete in teams to destroy the opposing team's base.

Dota 2

Dota 2 is a free-to-play MOBA game developed by Valve Corporation. In Dota 2, players control powerful heroes with unique abilities and work together in teams to destroy the enemy's Ancient building while defending their own.
